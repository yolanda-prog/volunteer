What is volunteering?

Volunteering is when you give your time to support an organisation or a community group. Volunteers are not paid. Some organisations may help with basic costs, such as transport.

For people who are new to Australia, volunteering can be a practical way to become part of the community and practise everyday English.

Why volunteer?

Volunteering has many benefits. Some of these are:

Learn new skills

Volunteering helps you learn useful skills for everyday life and work. Different roles involve different tasks. This experience can help you prepare for future jobs or training.

Improve your English skills

Volunteering gives you chances to talk with other people. Regular communication can help you practise spoken English and improve listening skills.

Meet new people and make friends

Volunteering can help you meet people from different backgrounds. You can make new friends and get to know others in the local community.

Experience Australian culture

By volunteering with local organisations, you can learn how community services and workplaces operate in Australia.

Support others and contribute to the community

Helping others can feel rewarding. Many people feel more connected and part of their community.
